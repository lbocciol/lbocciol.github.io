Luca Boccioli

https://orcid.org/0000-0002-4819-310X

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Physics at the University of Notre Dame. I earned my B.S. at the University of Perugia, where I also earned my M.S. My research interests mostly focus on Core-Collapse Supernovae, i.e. the explosion of massive stars. More specifically, I am interested in the role that General Relativity, the Nuclear Equation of State and neutrino physics have in triggering the explosion.

    CV

Most recent work

Currently I'm working on investigating the impact that the Nuclear Equation of state has on the explosion. Different Nuclear Equations of State (EOS) change the high-density phase of nuclear matter, and therefore the newly formed Proto-Neutron Star will evolve in a different thermodynamic state, depending on which Equation of state is used. What we find is that the entropy at the center of the star right after bounce is the quantity that best correlates with the strength of explosion. This has only partially been explored in other studies, which have only focused on one specific type of Equations of state (i.e. Skyrme-type EOS), whereas we expand on those studies by considering a wider range of EOS. See our paper for more information!
